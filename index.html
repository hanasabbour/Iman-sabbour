Iman Sabbour jewellery

Contemporary jewellery

Iman graduated from the school of fine arts, interior architecture department. She worked in several design firms for 9 years. Iman then joined the academic program of the Design Studio Azza Fahmy in 2017 and graduated in 2019. She worked as a teacher for some short courses at the DSAF. In 2020, she established her own studio.

As a student at the Design Studio Azza Fahmy, Iman exhibited her work in several local and international exhibitions. In 2018 She participated in the silver schools exhibition at legnica jewellery festival in Poland. The school has conceived a special exhibition to show the power of jewellery as a catalyser for reflection on time and meaning. Each student designed three rings: a ring for the past , a ring for the present and a ring for the future , consisting of three parts: an interactive installation of black wax rings inviting the visitors to add their own , a video installation and a series of rings each in a dialogue with an artist book.

In May 2019, she participated among her colleagues from the dsaf (Design Studio Azza Fahmy) in ATHENS JEWELRY WEEK, where they showcased their pieces under the theme titled "The Gift". ATHENS JEWELRY WEEK is an institution that creates the space where artists and audiences from the Contemporary Jewelry field can meet and exchange ideas, network and develop future collaborations. The works exhibited during AJW are art pieces that express states of being, provoke discussions and comment on current sociological structures and tendencies.

In 2018, the Design Studio Azza Fahmy collaborated with Mix & Match to design jewellery pieces for their spring/summer collection which theme was Siwa Oasis. Iman was part of this collaboration, she designed earrings that were inspired by the silver amulets used in the traditional “Aghaiz Nesalhat” necklaces of Siwa, and they were chosen among 3 other pieces to be displayed at their shops and also sold in MITCHA.
